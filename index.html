Rho Gamma Signature Programs

Sleep-Out for the Homeless

Every year in January, Rho Gamma hosts a food/clothes drive for the homeless. We sleepout under Lynch breeze way from 7pm until 7am. We collect canned goods, clothes, and money and donate them the following morning. We actively engage the campus and ask them to donate all night long. Students are asked to hangout or even get there friends to donate.

Afro-Latinx-Asian Night

Every year Rho Gamma hosts a program to show off and connect the diverse populations of UNCC. Each year we add another culture/religion /or ethnic group to bring something new to the table. Groups have taught traditional dances, history, games, and music. Come and expand your mind and circle of friends!

Sigma Beta Club

Year round Rho Gamma helps its graduate chapter (Beta Rho Sigma/Sigmas of Charlotte), mentor, tutor, and develop Phi Beta Sigma's youth auxillary group, the Sigma Beta Club. We take them on college tours, help with homework, and get involved in their everyday to help, mold, and direct the next generation of young men.

Rho Gamma History

The Rho Gamma Chapter of Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity Incorporated was founded on the campus of UNC at Charlotte March 14, 1988, by two men, W. L. Harris and K. S. Smith.

Since our founding Rho Gamma has taken pride in molding and inspiring men in the Charlotte community to strive for excellence. The men of Rho Gamma boast professional men in all fields of study and work including; Banking, divinity, law, I.T., entrepreneurship, community leaders, military, and much more. Reaching above and beyond is something Rho Gamma pushes brothers to do everyday.

Rho Gamma just doesn't inspire brothers to work to become better individually, we also use that excellence in combination with brotherhood to build a strong chapter. Rho Gamma boasts the title of 2x UNCC Fraternity of the Year (15-16 & 16-17). Along with that we have collected many other awards in Diversity, Prevention, and Non-Greek Programming.
